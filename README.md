# Marketing Budget Optimization

## Project Overview
This project develops a decision-support optimization model for allocating a fixed marketing budget across multiple advertising channels. The goal was to move beyond simple rule-of-thumb allocation and instead build a structured, data-driven framework that quantifies trade-offs across channels and recommends a budget mix that improves expected return.

The project is especially relevant to business-facing analytics roles because it focuses on translating a commercial question into a repeatable analytical solution: given limited resources, how should a company allocate spend to maximize business value?

---

## Business Problem
Marketing teams often need to decide how to allocate a constrained budget across multiple channels while balancing performance, diminishing returns, and practical business constraints. In many organizations, these decisions are made through historical habit, stakeholder preference, or fragmented point-in-time analysis.

This project reframed that challenge as a structured optimization problem. Instead of asking which channel performed best in isolation, the analysis focused on a more useful business question:

**How should a fixed budget be allocated across channels to maximize expected ROI while respecting real-world spending constraints?**

---

## Analytical Objective
The analytical objective was to build a quantitative optimization model that:

- allocates a fixed marketing budget across multiple advertising platforms,
- incorporates channel-level return assumptions,
- compares allocation strategies under business constraints,
- and produces a recommendation that can support managerial decision-making.

This makes the project relevant for roles that require rapid, decision-ready analytics rather than purely theoretical modeling.
